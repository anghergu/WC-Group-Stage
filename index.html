How ties are broken ›
Within a group, teams level on points are separated by, in order:
  1. Head-to-head points (matches between the tied teams only)
  2. Head-to-head goal difference
  3. Head-to-head goals scored
  4. Overall goal difference
  5. Overall goals scored
  6. Fair play score (cards)
Third-placed teams across groups never met, so there is no head-to-head. They rank by points, goal difference, goals scored, then fair play.

FIFA's actual final tiebreaker is the world ranking, then a drawing of lots. You asked to ignore the ranking, so that step is removed: any teams still dead level on every result are marked with = and left tied rather than ordered by ranking. Turn on Tiebreakers to enter cards (yellow −1, red −4 per team) if you want fair play to settle a tie.
